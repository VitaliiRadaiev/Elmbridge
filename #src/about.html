The Elmbride Mencap Story

In 1946 the late Judy Fryd, who received the M.B.E. for her services to Royal Mencap, wrote a letter to Nursery World magazine asking for other people in the same situation as her (caring for a mentally handicapped child) to get in touch to try and make their childrenâ€™s life more interesting. A tabloid newspaper ran the story and she was overwhelmed with the response she received. Along with three other mothers, they formed The Association of Parents of Backward Children.

In 1955 this was changed to The National Society for Mentally Handicapped Children, and again later the word Adults was added. 1955 was also the year that the Walton and Weybridge branch, later to be Elmbridge Mencap, was formed by Elsie and Tom Baker who lived in Walton. Note, that although affiliated to Royal Mencap, Elmbridge Mencap work independently at a local level. We receive no financial support from Royal Mencap.

Meet the team and volunteers

Elmbridge Mencap is mainly run by volunteers who devote a lot of time to fundraising through organising Christmas Fairs, Jumble Sales and Cinema Nights. A number of staff are employed to assist with the running of the hall, club organisation and management of the Day Service.
